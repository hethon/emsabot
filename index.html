EMSA

Ethiopian Medical Students’ Association. EMSA-St.Paul’s is a local chapter of EMSA-Ethiopia, which is a full member of the International Federation of Medical Students’ Associations(IFMSA).

By providing numerous training opportunities, professional and research exchange programs as well as platforms where members can assume administrative roles, EMSA allows its members to grow into holistic physicians, capable of using their knowledge and capacities for the benefit of society.

As a local chapter of one of the full members of the IFMSA, EMSA-St.Paul’s is uniquely positioned to utilize the resources made available by both EMSA-Ethiopia and IFMSA both in training or national and international research and professional exchange opportunities. EMSA-St.Paul’s collaborates with St.Paul’s Hospital in various health related campaigns, training and activities. It also collaborates with other various governmental and non-governmental organizations in its quest to realize its objective.

By subscribing to our channel- EMSASPH -you can stay up-to-date on the latest opportunities provided by our association or any of our stakeholders.

Membership and Active participation

You can become a member of EMSA-St.Paul’s by registering under any of our standing committees either during their call out or at any point in time by contacting the (vice)director of the standing committee of your choice. You are also automatically considered as a member of EMSA-St.Paul’s if you have an official position in EMSA-St.Paul’s as a member of the executive board, supervising council or the executive board’s assistants. Furthermore, students who have official positions in EMSA-Ethiopia or IFMSA are automatically considered as members of EMSA-St.Paul’s. However, following the official launch of EMSA-St.Paul’s members’ registry, one needs to be registered on the database to be officially acknowledged as EMSA-St.Paul’s member.

Joining a standing committee of your choice and partaking in the numerous projects they execute is one way to actively participate in the association. Moreover, you can opt to apply to be part of the task force for EB projects run under the association’s executive board when these projects are launched. Another way to actively participate in EMSA is by becoming an official with in the association.

EMSA-St.Paul’s Officials

The supervising council- supervises and advises the Executive Board and other EMSA-St.Paul’s Officials. The executive board- the president and his/her vices, are tasked with overseeing the workings of the standing committees. The Executive Board is entitled to officially represent the association. The Executive Board can delegate tasks to other persons, but it will remain responsible for these tasks at all times. Standing committee directors and vice directors- head their respective standing committees within their specific scope to execute, coordinate and support the activities of the association.

The executive board and the supervising council are elected by the general assembly, each body serving for a term lasting 1 year and a minimum of 1 year respectively. The directors and vices of the standing committees are appointed by the executive board following a thorough application process. They shall remain in position for 1 full term, unless reelected.

Members of the executive board can only be re-elected once. Standing committee officials can run and hold positions as many times as they can.

Standing Committees

  • The Standing Committee on Medical Education (SCOME)
  • The Standing Committee on Professional Exchange (SCOPE)
  • The Standing Committee on Public Health (SCOPH)
  • The Standing Committee on Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights including HIV and AIDS (SCORA)
  • The Standing Committee on Research Exchange (SCORE)
  • The Standing Committee on Human Rights and Peace (SCORP)
  • The Standing Committee of Information and Communication (SCOIC)
  • The Standing Committee of Personal Development (SCOPD)

You can apply for the standing committee of your choice during members’ callout. You may also contact the director or vice director of the standing committee of your choice and have your membership application considered. However, the decision will be left for the officials of the standing committee to either accept or reject your application taking the number of available projects , the capacity of the standing committee to accept new members as well as the strength of your candidature in to consideration.

You can join up to 2 standing committees simultaneously.
